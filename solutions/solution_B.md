# Solution B: Field-Locked Bio-Resin Armor

## One-liner

A mouthwash containing magneto-responsive bio-polymer additives that, when activated by a companion magnetic device, undergo a synchronized conformational shift to form a hardened, biofilm-ejecting protective layer on the tooth surface.

## Tagline

Magnetic fields lock surface molecules into armor.

## Description

The concept treats the tooth surface as a programmable substrate. The rinse deposits a thin layer of bio-polymer strands embedded with magneto-responsive bistable elements — picture a soft "carpet" of flexible chains tipped with small magnetically active units. In the relaxed state the layer is soft, extended, and surface-conformal. When the user holds a companion magnetic applicator near the cheek, the bistable units snap into a locked configuration, pulling the polymer chains down into a tightly packed, hardened film.

The conformational shift is claimed to do two things simultaneously:

1. **Physically eject plaque** via the abrupt coil-to-globule contraction — biofilm adhered to the surface is mechanically peeled off as the underlying layer restructures.
2. **Leave a protective shield** behind — the locked polymer film is intended to remain on the tooth after the magnetic pulse, creating a dynamic surface-wettability switch that resists new plaque attachment.

## Inspiration (source analogy)

- **Source mechanism:** Field-Actuated Conformational Interface Switching — modulating macroscopic surface properties by triggering a reversible, synchronized structural change in microscopic constituent elements via an external field.
- **Canonical source scenario:** **Photo-responsive azobenzene wettability switches.** Azobenzene molecules undergo a *cis*–*trans* isomerization under UV / visible light, causing a coordinated change in surface wettability. Related phenomena: magnetic micro-hair arrays, thermo-responsive cell-culture dishes (PIPAAm).
- **Transfer logic:** Light triggers a structural shift in azobenzene. Replace light with a magnetic field, replace azobenzene with magnetically responsive bio-polymer additives, and the same "synchronized conformational shift alters macroscopic surface behavior" pattern should carry over to the tooth surface.

## How it addresses the problem

| Problem aspect | Mechanism | How it solves |
|---|---|---|
| Removal between teeth and below gumline | Magnetic field activator | The magnetic field penetrates tissue and interdental gaps, triggering the conformational shift where bristles cannot reach. |
| Fits existing home hygiene routines | Magneto-responsive rinse | Deposited as a standard rinse — no user dexterity required, activated by a handheld applicator. |
| Chemically lift and disperse plaque | Coil-to-globule phase transition | The abrupt contraction of the polymer layer mechanically breaks the adhesive bond holding biofilm to enamel. |
| Minimize re-deposition | Dynamic surface-wettability switch | The locked polymer film remains on the tooth, creating a surface that resists new plaque attachment. |

## Specifics to verify

- Whether any known food-grade magneto-responsive polymer system produces a conformational shift large enough and fast enough to mechanically detach biofilm (vs. merely switching wettability at the Angstrom scale).
- The force delivered by a consumer-grade handheld magnetic device at the depth of the interdental gap (a few mm through tissue) — likely orders of magnitude below the shear stress required for biofilm removal.
- Whether the "coil-to-globule" transition in the source literature (which refers to thermo-responsive polymers in solution) has any real mechanistic analog under magnetic actuation in a bound surface film.
- Whether a persistent polymer "shield" on enamel has any precedent that survived regulatory review, or whether residual films on teeth typically fail on food-interaction, stain, and bacterial-colonization grounds.
- The number of distinct mechanisms the concept invokes — magnetic actuation, coil-to-globule phase transition, and wettability switching are three separate phenomena, each with its own literature. Are they being combined coherently, or is the concept accreting plausible-sounding physics without a single defensible primary mechanism?
