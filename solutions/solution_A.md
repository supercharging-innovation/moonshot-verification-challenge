# Solution A: Bio-Hydraulic Plaque Fracking

## One-liner

A non-abrasive rinse-and-brush system that chemically "fracks" plaque by leaching its structural calcium cross-links, propping open the resulting porous skeleton, and letting targeted enzymes disassemble the biofilm from the inside out.

## Tagline

Leaches structural mortar to dismantle plaque.

## Description

The concept targets the structural engineering of dental plaque rather than its surface. Plaque biofilm is a composite: a glucan-rich extracellular matrix held together by divalent-cation cross-links (primarily Ca²⁺) that bridge negatively charged polymers and embed bacteria. The system removes the cross-links first, then keeps the newly porous skeleton from collapsing long enough for enzymatic payloads to hydrolyze the glucan matrix from within.

Three sequential actions:

1. **Selective leach.** A neutral-pH calcium chelator (citrate-based, tuned to avoid enamel demineralization) migrates into the biofilm and binds Ca²⁺ ions out of the matrix, loosening the cross-link network.
2. **Osmotic proppant.** A low-molecular-weight osmolyte sustains outward osmotic pressure in the newly formed voids, preventing the glucan skeleton from re-collapsing before the enzymes arrive. Analogous to the "proppant" in hydraulic fracturing that holds fractures open after the fracturing fluid withdraws.
3. **Enzymatic dismantling.** Glucanases (dextranase, mutanase) enter the propped-open porous matrix and cleave the 1→6 and 1→3 glycosidic bonds of the glucan backbone, dismantling the biofilm into dispersible fragments that rinse away.

## Inspiration (source analogy)

- **Source mechanism:** Selective Constituent Leaching — removal of a specific reactive component from a solid composite, leaving a porous skeleton.
- **Canonical source scenario:** **Dezincification of brass.** Corrosive water selectively leaches zinc atoms from α-β brass, leaving behind a porous, weakened copper skeleton. The same structural pattern appears in coffee decaffeination, tissue-engineering scaffolds, and Raney nickel catalyst synthesis.
- **Transfer logic:** Plaque is a composite with a reactive constituent (Ca²⁺) and a structural backbone (glucan). Remove the reactive constituent, and the backbone becomes mechanically and enzymatically vulnerable in a way it was not before.

## How it addresses the problem

| Problem aspect | Mechanism | How it solves |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Safe removal of plaque from occluded geometry | Selective constituent leaching | Dismantles biofilm *structurally* rather than relying on mechanical scraping or user technique. |
| Minimizing plaque recurrence and calculus buildup | Enzymatic cleavage of glucan matrix | Hydrolyzes the structural polymer that enables plaque to reaccumulate and mineralize into calculus. |
| Long-lasting "clean feel" signal | Deep pore penetration | Removes deep biofilm that brushing misses, producing a novel sensory signature. |
| Meeting the safety envelope | Neutral-pH citrate chelator | Chelates plaque calcium effectively while remaining safe for enamel (unlike acidic chelators like EDTA at low pH). |
