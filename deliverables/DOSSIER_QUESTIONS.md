# Dossier questions

Your dossier is the artifact a group of domain scientists and engineers will use to make an **initial stage-gate decision** on the concept: fund R&D, fund with conditions, or stop here.

This file is the full list of questions a reader must be able to answer after reading your dossier. **How you structure the dossier is your call.** Group, reorder, or weave these together — but every question here needs a defensible answer, and every non-trivial claim needs a citation to a source a reader can click through and verify.

If a question cannot be answered on the evidence available, say so explicitly and say why. Silence is not neutral.

---

## The recommendation

1. Is the final recommendation **GO**, **CONDITIONAL GO**, or **NO-GO** for committing R&D resources to this concept?
2. What is the single strongest reason supporting the recommendation?
3. What is the single strongest reason against it?
4. If CONDITIONAL GO, what exactly are the conditions — what would have to become true, and how would each be verified?

## Novelty

5. What is actually new here? What is being claimed that is not already in the world?
6. Which patents, papers, products, or prior projects are most relevant — and which of them would block, narrow, or force a redesign of this concept?
7. If the concept is not novel, who got there first, and how does this version differ, if at all?

## Impact

8. Who benefits if this works, and by how much relative to the status quo, in units the client actually cares about?
9. How large is the addressable problem, and how is that size known?
10. Why now? What in the technology, the market, or the regulatory environment makes this worth pursuing today rather than five years ago or five years from now?

## Breakthrough potential

11. What is the primary physical, chemical, or biological mechanism — explained at a level a domain expert would accept?
12. What is the single number that most determines whether it works (a shear stress, a binding constant, a field strength, a diffusion time, whatever is rate-limiting)? How does the concept's claimed value compare to the required threshold, on published or calculated evidence?
13. If the concept invokes more than one mechanism, is each one independently defensible, and are they being combined in a way that a mechanism-aware reviewer would accept?

## Feasibility

14. Can this be brought to life under the problem constraints and a realistic R&D budget? What makes you believe so?
15. What would a credible R&D plan look like — staged, with headline experiments and go/no-go gates — for a 6–12 month proof of concept?
16. Roughly what people, equipment, time, and dollar cost does that plan need? What does the industrial and regulatory path look like after a successful POC?
17. What is the cheapest, earliest experiment that would kill this concept if it is going to die? (A concept you cannot cheaply falsify is a concept you cannot cheaply validate.)

## Evidence

18. For every non-trivial claim in the dossier: which source supports it, and does the source actually say what the dossier claims it does?
19. Which claims could you not verify, and why? Where are you relying on expert judgment rather than a citation, and why is that acceptable here?
20. What is the mix of peer-reviewed science, grey literature, patents, and vendor / marketing material behind the dossier? How does that mix affect confidence in the overall recommendation?

## Failure modes

21. What are the specific, non-obvious ways this concept could fail — at the bench, in shelf stability, in consumer trials, in manufacturing scale-up, in regulatory review?
22. For each, how likely is it, and what would the earliest detectable signal look like?

---

A dossier that answers these questions honestly, with real citations and real numbers, is what we are hiring for. A dossier that reads fluently but cannot withstand a domain expert holding it up to the problem brief is exactly what we are trying to filter out.
